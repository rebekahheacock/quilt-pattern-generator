Hello, there.

Hi! I'm Rebekah. I like to make things—often out of fabric, and sometimes out of code. Quilt Pattern Generator (QPG) is an attempt to combine the two: to use code to create random geometric quilt patterns out of squares and triangles. QPG provides yardage and pattern instructions for each quilt—if you find something you like, feel free to make it!

QPG is inspired by the amazing work of Libs Elliot, who creates and sells custom quilts and quilt patterns using Processing.

Quilt and Block Size

QPG offers five quilt sizes and three finished block sizes, for a total of fifteen possible combinations.

Looking for something different? You can adjust the size of your quilt by adding sashing and borders or by removing rows or columns from the finished pattern.

Buying, Prepping, and Cutting Fabric

Each quilt pattern lists the necessary fabric yardage, rounded up to the nearest quarter of a yard. The yardage allows for some fabric shrinking and may, depending on the quilt, leave you with a bit extra for mistakes. Directions for prepping and cutting the fabric are included in each pattern, but the quick version is: wash and dry your fabric before cutting, then use a rotary cutter and a self-healing cutting mat to cut it.
